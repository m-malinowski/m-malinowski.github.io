Conclusion

R003 now gives a complete four-panel audit, not only the motional-frequency audit, and it identifies the source of the optical-qubit-frequency discrepancy.

The full reproduction uses two 3-level ions coupled to a truncated motional Fock mode (NF=8), the Supp. Mat. S3 / thesis Eq. 9.15 MS Hamiltonian, the paper parameters δ = 2π × 15 kHz, η = 0.028, Ω = δ/(2η), and 80 protocol cycles. Repump B and drive C are applied to the full spin-motion density matrix, so residual spin-motion entanglement is not discarded. The second rectangular pulse uses the correct absolute Hamiltonian time offset, so the φ_m2 = π − δt phase cancels only the ideal δt phase and leaves the expected extra ε_m t phase when a motional-frequency error is present.

That full persistent spin-motion model reproduces the motional-frequency, Rabi-frequency, and Markovian-dephasing conclusions, but it does not reproduce the low-offset optical-qubit-frequency blue curve from the thesis excerpt. At ε_q / 2π = 0.25 kHz, the direct NF=8 diagnostic gives:

optical-qubit-frequency convention 80-cycle singlet error
persistent spin-motion through all cycles 3.59e-3
reset motion after each complete two-pulse drive A 4.62e-5
reset motion after each rectangular pulse 4.48e-3
phase-only correlated ground-state Z surrogate 2.17e-5

This explains the discrepancy with the user-provided thesis value of about 2e-5: that value is reproduced by the phase-only/correlated-phase model described in the text, and approximately by discarding motion after each complete two-pulse A step, but not by carrying the residual spin-motion state forward. The previous R003 statement that the optical-qubit-frequency panel was reproduced by the full persistent model was therefore too strong. The paper source says the simulation uses Eq. S6, but the plotted low-error optical-qubit curve behaves like the first-order “Bell phase only” explanation at source/supp_content.tex:109, not like the full persistent spin-motion propagation.

This fixes the earlier R003 omission and preserves the previous non-motional findings: the Rabi-error panel remains a strong match, and the dephasing panel still shows no protocol advantage. The motional-frequency discrepancy is also resolved: the old local-clock model artificially canceled residual displacement, while the corrected absolute-clock model makes the protocol curve rise as in the paper/thesis figure.

The verdict remains partial with a mismatch limitation. Three of the four panel conclusions are reproduced by the full persistent model, while the optical-qubit-frequency panel requires a phase-only/reset-after-A convention that is not stated explicitly enough in the paper text or thesis excerpt.
